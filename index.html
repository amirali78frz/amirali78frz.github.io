// Background

About Me

I am a PhD candidate at the Australian National University (ANU), supervised by Prof. Iman Shames and Dr. Philipp Braun. Currently, I am also a visiting student and researcher at the University of Melbourne, Department of Electrical and Electronics Engineering.

My research focuses on developing stochastic and zeroth-order optimisation algorithms for AI and machine learning, with a particular emphasis on min-max problems, saddle point theory, and adversarial training for robust ML systems.

I am a member of both the CIICADA Lab at ANU — a research group advancing control, information, and autonomous systems — and the DINGO Lab at the University of Melbourne, where I work on stochastic optimisation methods for large-scale AI problems.

Before starting my PhD, I completed a B.S. in Mechanical Engineering at Sharif University of Technology, with a focus on automatic control systems. I bring an interdisciplinary approach to my work, combining mathematical rigour with computational efficiency.

Zeroth-Order Optimisation · Ongoing
A deterministic variance-free zeroth-order framework based on exact cubature
Building a novel deterministic variance-free zeroth-order framework based on exact cubature leveraging spherical designs.
Zeroth-Order Optimisation
Closing the ZO-FO Gap via Input-to-State Stability
Analysing the conditions under which ZO algorithms can be formulated as bounded perturbed dynamical systems of their FO counterparts, to determine when they incur no extra dimension dependence in their iteration count — with direct implications for stochastic AI and ML at scale.
Zeroth-Order Minimisation
Quasar-convex, Polyak-Łojasiewicz, and Submodular Function Minimisation
Developing convergence guarantees for minimisation of structured nonconvex functions using stochastic gradient-free oracles, with applications to ML training pipelines.
Min-Max Optimisation
Nonconvex-Nonconcave and Submodular-Concave Min-Max via Stochastic Zeroth-Order Methods
Designing practical stochastic zeroth-order extragradient algorithms with theoretical convergence guarantees for adversarial and robust AI/ML scenarios.
Saddle Point Theory
Fixed Points, Continuous Flows, and Convergence to Saddle Points
Building a unified framework linking discrete-time minimax algorithms to continuous-time dynamical systems, with guarantees of convergence only to saddle points — foundational for stochastic ML training stability.
Raceline Optimisation · Ongoing
Raceline Generation for Autonomous Racing GitHub ↗ PyPI ↗
Developing a Python package and dataset for raceline optimisation; training neural networks to predict optimal velocity profiles from track map inputs. Ranked 5th in F1TENTH Sim Racing League at CDC 2024.
